Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu (BJJ)

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, also known as BJJ or Gracie Jiu-Jitsu, is a matial art and a fighting sport of which techniques are derived from judo and traditional jiu-jitsu. BJJ is based on ground fighting techniques and was developed by Gracie family in Brazil. The techniques used are chokehold (or strangulation), joint locks and muscle compressions, to subdue the opponent.
This discipline can be practised with or without keikogi (wrongly called a kimono). The no-gi practise (without keikogi) is similar to grappling.

My krav-maga club offers BJJ courses, in partnership with the Team Bittan Academy (TBA).
